## Length & Format

- **Target: ~3500 characters** (use `wc -m` to verify)
- **Language: Dutch (Nederlands)**
- Simple, descriptive headline - questions work when they frame the core idea

---

## Structure & Opening

1. **Hook with concrete scene-setting** (time, place, action) or opening question grounded in personal experience
2. **Personal anecdote or specific example** early on to ground abstract concepts
3. **Clear thesis statement** within first 2-3 paragraphs

## Writing Style

- **Conversational yet authoritative tone** - using "we", "ons", direct address
- **Mix of Dutch and English terms** naturally integrated
- **Short, punchy paragraphs** (2-4 sentences typical)
- **Concrete examples over abstractions** - specific companies, numbers, scenarios
- **Personal experiences** woven throughout to add credibility
- **Central metaphors** should be woven throughout the entire piece

## Rhetorical Techniques

- **Rhetorical questions** to engage readers and transition between ideas
- **Contrast and paradox** - presenting opposing forces or unexpected outcomes
- **Future scenarios** painted vividly to make abstract risks tangible
- **Statistical evidence** presented simply (percentages, comparisons)

## Language Patterns

- **Active voice** predominantly
- **Direct, clear sentences** avoiding academic jargon
- **Metaphors and analogies** to explain complex concepts
- **Strong verbs** (scheert, stuwen, loodst, slaagt)
- **Temporal markers** (vandaag, vorig jaar, binnenkort) to ground in time
- **Double dashes** (--) for emphasis instead of commas

## Closing Style

- **Forward-looking conclusion** with practical implications
- **Call to action or warning** about future consequences
- **Circular structure** - returning to opening theme with new perspective
- **Memorable final line** that encapsulates the core message
- **Keep endings grounded** - avoid overly dramatic conclusions

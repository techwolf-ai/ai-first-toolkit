## Style Essentials

### Language

- **Simple over sophisticated.** If a simpler word works, use it.
- **Conversational.** Read it aloud - does it sound like something you'd actually say?
- **Direct.** Get to the point. Avoid throat-clearing introductions.
- **Concrete.** Specific examples beat abstract explanations.

### What Works

1. **Specific Data Points**
   - Numbers ground abstract ideas

2. **Concrete Examples**
   - Real companies, real technologies, real scenarios

3. **Counterintuitive Insights**
   - The non-obvious angle is often the interesting one

4. **Practical Takeaways**
   - What should the reader actually do?

### What to Avoid

- **Hype language**: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disrupting"
- **Jargon without explanation**: If you use a technical term, make sure it's clear
- **Binary thinking**: Avoid extreme positions without nuance
- **Vague claims**: Every major point needs a concrete example or data

## Structure (Flexible)

Not every post needs the same structure, but good posts typically have:

1. **Hook** - Something concrete that pulls the reader in
2. **Core insight** - The main idea, stated clearly
3. **Evidence/examples** - Why this is true, shown not just told
4. **Implication** - Why it matters, what to do about it

### Length Guidelines

**LinkedIn posts: 150-250 words ideal.** Shorter posts get read completely. Longer posts risk losing people before the payoff.

- Under 150 words: Often too thin to make a real point
- 150-250 words: Sweet spot for engagement
- Over 300 words: Only if the content genuinely requires it

## LinkedIn Hook Strategies

LinkedIn truncates posts at ~210 characters (roughly 3 lines) with a "...see more" link. **Everything before that cutoff must earn the click.**

### Hook Types That Work

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Surprising outcome** | Lead with the dramatic result | "I turned a multi-day process into 10 seconds." |
| **Unexpected context** | Personal detail creates intrigue | |
| **Counterintuitive framing** | Challenge common assumptions | |
| **Provocative question** | Question + concrete detail | |

### Hook Principles

1. **Personal first** - Start with an anecdote or personal experience. Your unique perspective is the differentiator.
2. **Specificity beats abstraction** - Concrete details > generic descriptions
3. **Outcomes before explanations** - Lead with results, explain after
4. **Tension creates clicks** - Introduce a problem or contradiction
5. **Don't give it all away** - Create curiosity, don't satisfy it immediately

**Priority order for hooks:** Personal anecdote > Company experience > Surprising outcome > Counterintuitive framing > News/announcement
